A proper clean for floors that need more than a mop

Mopping can move soil around, but it often struggles with grout lines, textured tiles, greasy kitchen build-up and traffic marks near entrances. A professional clean focuses on loosening the soil, rinsing it away and leaving the floor looking brighter and easier to maintain.

Pre-inspection firstThe floor type, coating, grout condition and problem areas are checked before cleaning starts.
Suitable method for the surfaceCleaning strength and agitation are chosen to suit the material rather than treating every floor the same.
Cleaner edges and grout linesAttention is paid to the places that usually hold onto dirt, including edges, grout, corners and doorway traffic lanes.
Important: hard floor cleaning depends on the type of floor, the condition of the surface and whether previous sealers, coatings or residues are present. James will give honest advice on what can realistically be improved before work is carried out.

How the hard floor cleaning process works

Inspect

The floor is checked for type, condition, problem areas, coatings and any risks before cleaning.

Pre-treat

Suitable cleaning solution is applied to help loosen soil, grease, residue and marks.

Agitate

Edges, grout lines and textured areas are worked carefully to release embedded dirt.

Rinse and finish

The loosened soil is removed and the floor is left cleaner, brighter and more presentable.
